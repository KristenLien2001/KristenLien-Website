I am a current student at University of California, Riverside majoring in English. I am hardworking, responsible, an effective oral and written communicator, and love to challenge myself personally and professionally. My passion is storytelling and the power of language; if you see me on campus, I'm probably reading. Ever since I was young there has been nothing more satisfying than disappearing into a well written story and immersing myself into a different world. I am a life long learner, and I am always eager to learn something new everyday. I love meeting new people and exposing myself to different perspectives, and I have found that writing is one of the best ways to do so.Currently, I am a prose editor at Mosaic, the university's literary magazine. My responsibilities include: reading weekly submissions to the magazine (about 110 pages per week), attending meetings to discuss the submissions, community outreach for the writing community of Riverside, and deciding which submissions will be published in the magazine. I love being able to read stories from current students, contributing to Riverside's budding community of writers and poets, and getting to publish the works of young, talented writers. I believe that as an editor, my job is to help writers find their voices and tell their stories in the best way they can.

In addition, I have 4 years of retail experience from the Los Angeles Arboretum where I greeted customers, efficiently processed transactions in a fast-paced environment, operated cash registers, computers, and scanners, and assisted with store stocking and floor-sets. I also have over 5 years of experience in an office setting at the medical office of Dr. Thoi Lien MD. During my time there I assisted in taking vitals of patients, inputted referrals into a database (Glacemr), filed patient documents, shredded papers, and cleaned the office daily. I also have 2 years of volunteer experience as a fundraising coordinator for the Southern California Teo Chew Association where I assisted with recruiting volunteers for the annual Lunar New Year fundraiser, priced merchandise, set up the fundraising stand, processed transactions and handled donations.

In my free time I like to exercise (I love kettlebells), hike around Riverside, travel, watch movies (my favorite film makers are David Lynch, Taika Waititi, and Don Hertzfeldt), and write. My current favorite novels/ short stories are I Have No Mouth and I Must Scream by Harlan Ellision, Little Fires Everywhere by Celeste Ng, and Brave New World by Aldous Huxley.
